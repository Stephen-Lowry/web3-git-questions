Question 2:

Compare CLI vs. GUI clients for working with Git


Answer:

CLI clients at a glance

>

Advantages of commandline clients

  • Very powerful and flexible
  • Cross-platform: works on all systems with no adjusting period
  • Learn once: cnce learnt, you don't have to learn new commands / procedures when you go another system
  • Can configure all parts of the program

Disadvantages of commandline clients


GUI clients at a glance

>

Advantages of GUI clients

  • Avoid having to learn off commands
  • Avoid having to type; point-and-click interface
  • Options are clearly and easily presented

Disadvantages of GUI clients

  • GUI clients differ from system to system and require an adjusting period
  • Not as powerful or flexible as their CLI counterparts
  • Not all options may be available and may not be able to configure all parts of the Git program

Comparative thoughts....

I believe the choice between the two client types comes down to three factors:
  1. Cross-platform use: if the developer is only working on one system then a GUI client is a very good choice.
  2. Commandline comfortability: if the developer is not happy with working from the terminal then a GUI would be strongly favoured.
  3. Variance of commands: GUI's are not as flexible and tend not to have all of the options available to the user; if the developer does not vary from the normal Git commands / uses too much then a GUI might be a better choice.

Personally, I prefer to work from the terminal; I have years of experience in a CLI environment, I am a very capable typist and I regularly work on different (operating) systems so cross-platform advantages appeal to me.
